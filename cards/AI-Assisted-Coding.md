Q: How should you view AI as a coding partner?
A: As a junior developer or coworker who learns iteratively through feedback.

---

Q: How should you interact with AI for coding?
A: In a feedback loop, refining prompts and providing context.

---

Q: How can new developers use AI to learn coding?
A: Through explanations and step-by-step guidance from the model.

---

C: [Context] is king when providing information to AI models.

---

Q: What type of context should you provide to AI models?
A: Concise, high-impact context rather than overwhelming amounts of information.

---

Q: When should you start new conversations with AI?
A: When tasks change.

---

Q: How to start a task with an AI Model ?
A: Start with a plan—use AI's reasoning and planning modes (e.g., Cloud Code's plan mode) to generate structured plans.

---

Q: How to break down tasks for AI?
A: Create task lists or use task-tracking tools (issues...).

---

Q: How to ensure safe experimentation with AI tools?
A: Use source control and checkpoints—maintain branching and rollback practices when experimenting with AI-generated code.

---

Q: What is the goal of AI when using it?
A: Use AI to augment human expertise, not replace it.

---

Q: Why use MCPs selectively?
A: To avoid unnecessary context bloat.

---

Q: What mindset should developers adopt for AI-assisted coding?
A: Pair programming mindset—leveraging AI as a learning and productivity partner while remaining the ultimate decision-maker.
